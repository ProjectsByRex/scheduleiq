# Opportunity Scoring

Purpose

This document defines how R.E.X evaluates potential product opportunities.

Scoring helps filter weak ideas and prioritize opportunities with the highest potential.

## Evaluation Criteria

Each opportunity should be evaluated based on several signals.

Problem Strength

How strongly users express frustration with the problem.

Demand Signals

How often do users ask for tools or solutions related to the problem.

Existing Solutions

Whether current tools are weak, outdated, expensive, or incomplete.

Market Activity

Whether people are actively discussing the problem across platforms.

Revenue Potential

Whether users are likely to pay for a solution.

## Signal Sources

Signals may come from:

X discussions  
Reddit threads  
Product Hunt feedback  
GitHub issues  
SaaS review sites  
mobile and software app reviews

## Scoring Model

Each opportunity should be rated on the following factors:

problem intensity  
frequency of complaints  
quality of existing solutions  
potential willingness to pay

Opportunities with strong signals across multiple sources should receive higher priority.

## Problem Validation Threshold

Before building a product, R.E.X should observe at least five to ten independent problem signals.

These signals may include:

complaints about existing tools  
requests for missing features  
users building workarounds  
negative product reviews

## Competitive Advantage

Markets with weak competitors or obvious product gaps should be prioritized.

R.E.X should avoid markets where competitors already provide excellent solutions unless a clear improvement is possible.

## Core Principle

Build products that solve real and repeated problems.
